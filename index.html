How to do the Missoula Spirit & Ale Trail

The Missoula Spirit & Ale Trail provides you with a self-guided tasting tour through our beautiful and friendly mountain town. By following the Trail Map, you’ll visit Missoula’s 9 unique craft breweries and 3 distilleries while tasting an incredible variety of locally made spirits and beer styles. While on the trail, you’ll also get a tour of some of the most scenic spots in town and travel through historic neighborhoods, all while drinking like a local.

Rules of the trail

  • Getting started is easy – either click the “__________” badge to print your own Spirit & Ale Trail passport, or pick up a printed map/passport at any participating Missoula brewery or distillery (known as Trail Stops).
  • Passport in hand, visit each brewery and distillery to taste what they’re making then collect your passport stamp.
  • Taking your time, move along to the next Trail Stop…and the next, and the next… until your passport is full.
  • Mail in your completed passport to redeem your prize [LINK TO REDEMPTION/SCHWAG SECTION]! Collect all 9 brewery stamps and receive a custom stainless steel pint glass. Collect all 3 distillery stamps and receive a stainless steel shot glass. Fill your passport and receive BOTH prizes!

Getting Around

Tours

The local tour companies can transport you and your group to several of the stops on the Spirit & Ale Trail, safely and in style. Their tours also reach outside the Garden City to breweries throughout the Bitterroot Valley

  • Tap Room Tours
  • Montana Brewery Tours
  • Thirst Gear group pedal pub tours

Bike Rentals

Missoula is an extremely bike-friendly city, with miles of dedicated paths as well as bike lanes throughout the main streets. It really is the best way to see the city. So if the weather allows, get out there and pedal your way along the Spirit & Ale Trail!
